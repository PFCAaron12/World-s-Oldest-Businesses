World’s Oldest Businesses Analysis


Overview

This project analyzes the world’s oldest businesses to understand their industries, geographic distribution, and factors contributing to long-term survival. The goal is to uncover patterns behind business longevity across countries and continents. 




Dataset

Tables: 3

Columns: 9 total

Rows: 377 total

Content:

Business details (e.g., founding year, country code)

Industry categories and codes

Country codes and names

Missing data: None detected. 


Data Preparation

Data was manually reviewed for:

Spelling errors and whitespace issues

Data type consistency

Null values (none found)
After cleaning, SQL queries were used for analysis in PostgreSQL. 


Key Findings

Founding year range: 578 AD to 1999.

Oldest company: Kongo Gumi (Japan, construction), notable for adapting products during economic downturns.

Pre-1000 AD companies: Concentrated in the Eastern world.

Common industries: Banking & Finance, followed by wineries and breweries due to historical necessity and cultural roles.

Continental trends: Africa contains the largest number of oldest companies, influenced by natural resources and colonial history.
